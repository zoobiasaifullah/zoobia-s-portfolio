What's Next

Research

I'm co-authoring a systematic literature review of over 50 studies to identify critical gaps in accessibility and clarity of captions in short-form videos. This research, part of a forthcoming ACM CHI 2026 submission, informs user interviews and eye-tracking studies to investigate how caption design influences digital equity and audience comprehension. The ultimate goal is to develop evidence-based guidelines that enhance caption quality, ensuring more inclusive and accessible digital content for all users.

AI in Design

I'm actively exploring how AI is shaping the future of design, from adaptive interfaces to intelligent user research workflows. By experimenting with emerging tools that support ideation, synthesis, and prototyping, I'm learning how to integrate AI into the UX process in ways that enhance creativity without replacing critical thinking. This journey is helping me stay ahead as the design landscape evolves.

About Me

I'm Zoobia, a third-year HCI student at NJIT's Albert Dorman Honors College with a passion for designing products that solve problems and feel natural to use.

My design process revolves around deep listening, noticing patterns, and genuinely connecting with people. I enjoy figuring out why someone abandons a checkout flow or hesitates before tapping a button, because understanding these moments are key to creating better experiences.

I pay close attention to small details and behaviors that often go unnoticed. I'm naturally reflective, always trying to grow, and driven by the hope that my work, even in small ways, can make someone's day a little better.
