What's Next

Research

I'm co-authoring a systematic literature review of over 50 studies to identify critical gaps in accessibility and clarity of captions in short-form videos. This research, part of a forthcoming ACM CHI 2026 submission, informs user interviews and eye-tracking studies to investigate how caption design influences digital equity and audience comprehension. The ultimate goal is to develop evidence-based guidelines that enhance caption quality, ensuring more inclusive and accessible digital content for all users.

About Me

I'm Zoobia, a third-year HCI student at NJIT's Albert Dorman Honors College. I spend a lot of time thinking about the small moments that shape how people experience technology‚Äîlike why someone might pause before hitting "submit" or what makes them trust (or distrust) an AI recommendation.

What drew me to UX was realizing that good design isn't about making things look nice‚Äîit's about understanding people. I get genuinely curious about the "why" behind user behavior. When I'm running usability tests or interviews, I find myself noticing the micro-hesitations, the slight confusion in someone's voice, the moment they light up because something just clicked. Those moments tell me more than any metric could.

AI fascinates me not because it's trendy, but because it's fundamentally changing how we solve problems. I'm constantly experimenting with new AI tools, wondering how they could make research faster, prototyping smarter, or experiences more personalized without being creepy. I think the real challenge isn't building AI features‚Äîit's building ones that people actually understand and feel comfortable using.

I notice patterns that others miss‚Äîthe friction points users don't mention out loud, the way their frustration builds gradually, the gap between what stakeholders assume and what actually happens in practice. I ask a lot of questions because I'd rather dig deeper and solve the right problem than rush to implement something that looks good but doesn't quite work. Every project pushes me to get better at balancing user needs with business goals, and I'm genuinely excited about that challenge.
